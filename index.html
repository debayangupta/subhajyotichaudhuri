I'm a third-year doctoral student in the Batista Group at Yale University. Contact me at subhajyoti.chaudhuri@yale.edu.

I'm interested in the theoretical modeling of electron transfer, especially in light triggered functional materials. Before coming to Yale, I completed a BSc from the University of Calcutta and an MSc from Jadavpur University. During the latter, I interned at the Saha Institute of Nuclear Physics in India. I also worked as a junior research fellow at Jadavpur after my MSc.

Research

Sustainable production of energy and simultaneous preservation of the environment are among the primary challenges facing humanity today. Recent developments in functional materials hold great promise for developing novel solutions in this area (e.g., solar cells, light emitting diodes, etc.). In order to rationally design such materials a detailed insight into the underlying electron transfer (ET) mechanisms is required.

The aim of my proposed research is to develop a simple Density Functional Theory (DFT) based method for the computation of ET rates using the semi-classical Marcus Theory. Once this is achieved, I intend to use the technique to predict intra- and inter-molecular ET rates for a wide range of systems.
